The Indefinite Article

The indefinite article takes two forms.It’s the word awhen it precedes a word that beginswith a consonant.It’s the word an when it precedes a word that begins with a vowel.The indefinite articleindicates that a noun refers to a general idea rather than a particular thing.For example,you might ask your friend,“Should I bring a gift to the party?”.Your friend will understand that you are not asking about specific type of gift or a specific item.

“I am going to bring an apple pie,” your friend tells you.Again,the indefinite article indicates that she is not talking about a specific apple pie.Your friend probably doesn’t even have any pie yet.The indefinite article only appears with singular nouns.Consider the followingexamples of indefinitearticles used in contex.
